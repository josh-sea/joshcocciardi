Cornell 5/8/77 — still the greatest show ever played?
Posted in: Show Discussion  |  311 replies  |  Started by Rosebud_Mary on March 3, 2025
🌹
Rosebud_Mary
Senior Member | Posts: 4,211 | Joined: 2004
March 3, 2025 at 11:22 AM
I know this gets brought up every few months, but I was listening to the Cornell '77 board recording last night and I'm telling you — that Scarlet > Fire > Estimated transition in the second set is absolutely untouchable. 47 years later and it still gives me chills.

So let's have it out once and for all. Is Cornell 5/8/77 the best show they ever played? I'll accept arguments for Veneta '72, Watkins Glen, or Nassau '80 but you're going to have to convince me.

Drop your picks below with reasoning. Let's keep it civil, folks!
— Mary | "The music never stopped" | 🌹🌹🌹
🎸
JerryFan68
Legend | Posts: 12,440 | Joined: 2003
March 3, 2025 at 12:07 PM
Mary, you know I love you, but I have to go with Veneta 8/27/72. That "Dark Star" is from another dimension entirely. Cornell is magical but I think it gets outsized credit just because the soundboard is so pristine. Veneta sounds rough but the PLAYING is on another level.

That said — the Cornell "Morning Dew" is probably Jerry's single best guitar moment. So maybe it's a tie in different categories.
— Frank | Deadhead since 1968 | 🎸
🌀

I've listened to both probably 200 times each and I go back and forth. But today I'm saying Cornell. The energy in that room was just different. You can HEAR the audience on the recording and they knew they were witnessing something special in real time.

My hot take: the 1977 spring tour as a WHOLE is the peak of the Grateful Dead. Cornell is just the crown jewel.
— Casey | "Driving that train..." 🚂
